Best 30 / Worst 30 for Efficiency - July 2001

The best require the least effort to generate the required ball speed, therefore with a maximum effort they can hit the ball fastest and should be most powerful on the serve, provided that the stringing and other factors affecting racquet bounce (coefficient of restitution) are the same for all racquets. The worst require the most effort. The rankings under the five benchmark criteria were weighted according to the ratio of the magnitude of the Work required for the best racquet (lowest Work) in that benchmark condition to the Work required for the best in the Second Benchmark Condition, which was highest of all, and therefore by weighting had more effect on the composite weighted rank.
