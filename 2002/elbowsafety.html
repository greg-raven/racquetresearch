Elbow Safety -- 2002

The best (Yonex Muscle Power 3i) are the safest for the elbow; the worst (Wilson Triads) are not recommended. See what causes tennis elbow. The macro criterion of Elbow Safety comprises the evaluation criteria of 1.5*Elbow Crunch, Torque, Shock, 0.5*Moment, and 0.5*Flex. Moment contributes to Torsion, a possible cause of tennis elbow, and Flex may ameliorate Shock by damping vibration and Torque by increasing dwell time. These were weighted half as much as Torque and Shock, which are more important, and Elbow Crunch was weighted 1.5 times Torque and Shock, because it is even more important. Each of these evaluation criteria (Torque, etc.) produced composite rankings when the results under the five benchmark conditions were compiled, weighted, summed, and sorted. These composite rankings then were compiled, weighted, summed, and sorted to produce rankings under the macro criterion of Elbow Safety.
